03:00-05:30 PM

🌋 Beppu "Hells" Tour - Jigoku Meguri (別府地獄めぐり)

Duration: 2.5 hours | Spectacular geothermal hot springs - kids LOVE these!

  • What are "Hells"?: Boiling hot springs (98°C!) too hot for bathing - viewing only!
  • Hell Pass: ¥2,200/adult, ¥1,000/child (covers all 7 hells) - BEST VALUE!
  • Individual tickets: ¥450/hell (not recommended - pass saves ¥1,000+)
  • Operating hours: 8:00 AM-5:00 PM daily
  • Two main areas:
    • Kannawa Area (4 hells): Main cluster, easily walkable
    • Shibaseki Area (3 hells): 15 min drive away
  • 🌊 Umi Jigoku (Sea Hell): #1 must-see! Cobalt blue 98°C pool, beautiful gardens
  • 🩸 Chi no Ike Jigoku (Blood Pond Hell): Bright red hot spring - looks like blood!
  • 🌪️ Tatsumaki Jigoku (Tornado Hell): Geyser erupts every 30-40 min!
  • 👻 Oniyama Jigoku (Devil Mountain Hell): 80+ crocodiles heated by hot springs!
  • 🪨 Oniishibozu Jigoku: Bubbling gray mud pools (looks like shaved monk heads)
  • 🍳 Kamado Jigoku (Cooking Pot Hell): Multiple pools, foot bath included!
  • 🏔️ Shiraike Jigoku (White Pond Hell): Milky blue-white hot spring
  • 👧 Kid highlights: Crocodiles, onsen-boiled eggs (¥100), colorful pools!
  • Route suggestion: Start at Umi Jigoku (most beautiful), then others in Kannawa, drive to Blood Pond & Tornado
  • Time per hell: 15-20 min each (total 2-2.5 hours)
  • 🅿️ Parking: Free at each hell
  • 💰 Budget: ¥5,500-6,500 (passes + snacks + onsen eggs)
05:45-07:45 PM

  • Gas: If rental agreement requires full tank, gas up near hotel BEFORE going to airport

🗺️ Navigation tip: Set GPS to "福岡空港 国際線ターミナル" (Fukuoka Airport International Terminal)

06:30-07:00 AM

🚗 Return Rental Car

  • Location: Rental car return is SEPARATE from terminal - follow "レンタカー返却" signs
  • International Terminal return: Most companies have counters near Intl Terminal
  • Process:
    • 1) Park at designated return area
    • 2) Staff inspects car (5-10 min) - usually very quick, no damage issues
    • 3) Get receipt and confirm charges
    • 4) Walk or take free shuttle to terminal (2-3 min)
  • Items to remove: Check glove box, door pockets, trunk, under seats!
  • Thank you: Say "Osewa ni narimashita" (お世話になりました) = "Thank you for your help"

💡 Tip: Keep rental agreement until you're through security in case of any questions

07:00-08:00 AM

✈️ Check-in at International Terminal

  • Singapore Airlines counter: Level 3, rows A-F
  • Check-in:
    • Online: If you did online check-in, just drop bags at baggage counter
    • Counter: Show passports + booking confirmation
    • Baggage: 30kg per person for economy (Singapore Airlines)
    • Request: Family seats together, window for daughter if possible
  • Documentation:
    • Singapore arrival requirements (usually none for citizens, but check latest)
    • Have boarding passes ready
08:00-08:30 AM

🛂 Immigration & Security Screening

  • Japan exit procedures:
    • Immigration: Present passports, departure cards (if required)
    • Automated gates: Some nationalities can use e-gates (faster!)
    • Queue time: Usually 10-20 minutes in early morning
  • Security: Remove laptops, liquids in zip-lock bag, shoes off
  • Kids: Strollers allowed until gate (will be checked at aircraft door)

💡 Tip: Keep snacks and daughter's entertainment easily accessible in carry-on!

08:30-09:30 AM

🛍️ Last-Minute Duty-Free Shopping & Breakfast

  • Breakfast options at departure gates:
    • Mos Burger: Japanese burger chain - try teriyaki burger!
    • Cafe: Coffee, pastries, light breakfast
    • Onigiri stands: Rice balls for flight snack
    • Budget: ¥1,500-2,500 for family breakfast
  • Final souvenir shopping:
    • Royce Chocolate: Famous Hokkaido brand (nama chocolate melts, get the stable version!)
    • Tokyo Banana: Classic Japanese souvenir snack
    • Shiroi Koibito: White chocolate cookies
    • Fukuoka Mentaiko: Spicy cod roe (vacuum-packed for travel)
    • Sake/Whisky: Japanese alcohol duty-free (Hibiki, Yamazaki if available)
    • Kit-Kat flavors: Unique Japanese flavors (matcha, sake, regional specials)
    • Cosmetics: Japanese skincare/makeup brands duty-free
  • Currency: Use remaining yen! Credit cards accepted but cash preferred
  • Tax refund: If you did tax-free shopping, bring stamped documents

💰 Budget tip: Save ¥5,000-10,000 yen for duty-free treats! Prices often better than city stores.

09:30 AM

🎫 Boarding Begins

  • Gate: Check departure board for gate number (usually announced 45 min before departure)
  • Priority boarding: Families with young children can board early (ask gate agent!)
  • Bathroom break: One last restroom visit before boarding
  • Daughter prep: Change diaper if needed, have toys/iPad ready for takeoff
10:00 AM

✈️ Departure - Singapore Airlines SQ655

  • Aircraft: Boeing 787-10 Dreamliner (modern, comfortable!)
  • Departure: Fukuoka Airport (FUK) International Terminal
  • Destination: Singapore Changi Airport (SIN)
  • Flight time: 6 hours
  • Time zone: Singapore is 1 hour behind Japan (arrive 3:00 PM Singapore time = 4:00 PM Japan time)
  • In-flight service:
    • Lunch served ~1 hour after takeoff
    • Entertainment system with movies, games for kids
    • Singapore Airlines known for excellent service!
    • Request kids' meal if pre-ordered, otherwise regular meal fine

📱 Window seat tip: Great views of Japanese islands on takeoff, South China Sea during flight!

Essential Travel Tips

🚗 Driving in Japan

  • Drive on the LEFT side
  • Get International Driving Permit before trip
  • GPS: Request English GPS with rental car
  • Tolls: Highway tolls expensive (¥3,000-5,000 for longer trips)
  • Gas: Say "man-tan" for full tank
  • Parking: Most attractions have parking ¥300-800

👧 Traveling with Kids

  • Request child car seat from rental company
  • Convenience stores have clean bathrooms everywhere
  • Parks have excellent playgrounds - take breaks!
  • Many restaurants have kids menus
  • Coin laundry widely available
  • Bring snacks - kids get hungry!

☔ June Weather

  • Rainy season (tsuyu) - expect rain!
  • Pack rain jackets & umbrellas
  • Temperature: 20-28°C (68-82°F)
  • Humid - light, breathable clothing
  • Sunscreen still needed
  • Indoor backup activities planned

💴 Money & Payment

  • Cash is king - many places don't take cards
  • 7-Eleven ATMs accept foreign cards
  • Budget: ¥15,000-20,000/day for family
  • IC card (Suica/Pasmo) useful for trains
  • Tax-free shopping at major stores (passport needed)

📱 Connectivity

  • Rent pocket WiFi at airport
  • Or get tourist SIM card
  • Download Google Maps offline
  • Google Translate app is essential
  • Most hotels have free WiFi

🎒 Packing Essentials

  • ✅ Sunscreen and hats
  • ✅ Rain gear (jackets, umbrella)
  • ✅ Comfortable walking shoes
  • ✅ Swimwear (beach & onsen)
  • ✅ Small backpack for day trips
  • ✅ Portable phone charger
  • ✅ Basic first aid kit
