Day 1
June 14 (Sat)

Arrival & Settling In

Easy Day
01:20 AM

✈️ Depart Singapore (SIN)

Singapore Airlines SQ 656 | Boeing 787-10 | Economy Class

📍 Changi Terminal 2 → Fukuoka Intl Terminal I

⏱️ Flight Duration: 6h 0m | Arrival: 08:20 AM (Japan Time, GMT+9)

  • Pre-flight: Arrive at Changi T2 by 11:00 PM (check-in counter closes 40 min before departure)
  • Kids: Bring entertainment (iPad, coloring books) - 6-year-old will sleep ~3-4 hours
  • Meal service: Light supper after takeoff, breakfast before landing
08:20-09:30 AM

🛬 Arrive Fukuoka Airport (FUK)

Duration: ~70 min | Immigration → Baggage → Car Rental

  • Immigration: 20-30 min (family lane available)
  • Baggage claim: 10-15 min (Belt 1 or 2 for international)
  • Car rental: 30 min (recommend: Toyota Rent-a-Car or Times Car Rental)
  • 📝 Required: International Driving Permit + Singapore license + booking confirmation
  • 💰 Cost: ~¥8,000-12,000/day for compact/mid-size car with ETC card & GPS
  • 👶 Request: Child safety seat (usually free with advance booking)
10:00-11:30 AM

🚗 Drive to Hotel & Check-in

Duration: 15-20 min drive to Hakata/Tenjin area + check-in

  • Recommended hotels: Hakata area (near station) or Tenjin (shopping district)
  • Check-in: Most hotels allow early check-in from 10-11 AM if available
  • If room not ready: Leave luggage with concierge and explore
  • 👶 Freshen up: Kids can nap 30-60 min if needed
  • 🅿️ Parking: Most hotels: ¥1,500-2,000/night
12:00-01:00 PM

🍜 Lunch: Hakata Ramen

Duration: 1 hour | First meal in Fukuoka!

  • Ichiran Ramen Canal City: Famous solo booth dining (¥1,200/person) - kid-friendly!
  • Ippudo Hakata: Family-friendly tables, english menu (¥1,000-1,500/person)
  • Kids menu: Available at most ramen shops (½ portion + gyoza)
  • 💰 Budget: ¥3,000-4,000 for family of 3
02:00-05:30 PM

🏢 Canal City Hakata

Duration: 3-4 hours | Light exploration to stay awake and combat jet lag

  • Fountain shows: 10:00, 12:00, 14:00, 16:00, 18:00, 20:00 (12 min each) - Catch the 14:00 & 16:00 shows!
  • Kids activities: Toy stores (Kiddy Land), arcade games, anime shops
  • Playground: Small kids' play area on B1F (free)
  • Snacks: Get Japanese treats - pocky, melon bread, soft-serve ice cream
  • 🅿️ Parking: ¥200/30min (first hour free with ¥2,000 purchase)
  • 💰 Budget: ¥5,000-8,000 for shopping/snacks
  • 🚻 Facilities: Clean nursing rooms & family toilets on each floor
Visit Website →
06:00-07:30 PM

🍽️ Dinner at Canal City Food Court

Duration: 1.5 hours | Variety for everyone

  • Options: Tonkatsu, udon, tempura, sushi, okonomiyaki
  • Kid-friendly: Karaage (fried chicken), hamburg steak, curry rice
  • 💰 Budget: ¥3,500-5,000 for family
  • Dessert: Pablo Cheese Tart (Fukuoka specialty!) ~¥250
08:00 PM

🏨 Back to Hotel & Early Sleep

Combat jet lag: Early night essential for Day 2!

  • Convenience store run: Grab breakfast items, snacks, drinks for tomorrow
  • Bath time: Let daughter enjoy Japanese bathtub (onsen-style at home!)
  • Target sleep: 9:00-9:30 PM for everyone
  • ⏰ Set alarm: 7:00 AM for Day 2
💡 Pro Tips for Day 1:
  • Stay awake: Avoid napping until 8 PM to adjust to Japan time
  • Car rental insurance: Take full CDW coverage (~¥1,500/day extra but worth it)
  • Download apps: Google Maps (offline), Hyperdia (trains), Google Translate
  • Cash: Withdraw ¥30,000-50,000 from 7-Eleven ATM (accepts most cards)
  • ETC card: Must-have for highway tolls (saves ~30% vs cash)
  • Free WiFi pocket: Some car rentals offer it free - request at counter

💰 Day 1 Total Budget: ¥15,000-25,000 (~S$150-250) excluding hotel

09:30 AM-12:30 PM

🦢 Ohori Park (大濠公園)

Duration: 3 hours | Beautiful Japanese garden, swan boats, playground

📍 1-2 Ohorikoen, Chuo Ward | 🚗 10 min drive from Hakata
  • Swan boat rental: ¥600/30min (max 3 people) - Must-do for kids!
  • Operating hours: 10:00 AM-6:00 PM (boats)
  • Playground: Large modern playground near west entrance (free)
  • Japanese Garden: ¥250/adult, ¥120/child (6-15 years) - beautiful koi pond
  • Starbucks Fukuoka: Scenic lakeside location - perfect for coffee break
  • 🚻 Facilities: Multiple clean restrooms around the park
  • 🅿️ Parking: Ohori Park Station parking ~¥200/hour
  • 👶 Kid-friendly: Completely stroller-accessible, bring bread for fish feeding!
  • 💰 Budget: ¥1,500-2,500 (boats + garden + drinks)
12:30-01:30 PM

🍱 Lunch at Ohori Park Area

Duration: 1 hour

  • Boathouse Restaurant: Inside park, kid-friendly, pasta/curry (¥800-1,500)
  • Alternative: Picnic! Buy bento from nearby supermarket (~¥500-800 each)
  • Royal Host (family restaurant): 5 min drive, Western + Japanese menu
  • 💰 Budget: ¥2,500-4,000 for family
01:45-03:00 PM

🏯 Fukuoka Castle Ruins & Maizuru Park

Duration: 1.25 hours | 2-minute walk from Ohori Park!

  • Free entry - explore the castle ruins and ramparts
  • Easy walk: 15-20 min to reach observation deck
  • City views: Panoramic views of Fukuoka from castle tower base
  • History: Built 1601, only stone walls remain
  • 👶 Kid note: Some stairs, but manageable with 6-year-old
  • Photo spots: Stone walls, guardian statues
  • Duration: 45 min leisurely walk + photo time
03:30-05:00 PM

🏖️ Momochi Seaside Park & Fukuoka Tower

Duration: 1.5 hours | 15 min drive from castle

  • Beach time: Clean sandy beach - kids can play, paddle, collect shells
  • Fukuoka Tower entry: ¥800/adult, ¥500/child (4-15 yrs)
  • Tower hours: 9:30 AM-10:00 PM (last entry 9:30 PM)
  • Sky Lounge: 123m high - amazing sunset views around 6-7 PM in June
  • Skip tower?: If daughter is tired, just enjoy beach (free!)
  • 🅿️ Parking: Momochi Seaside Park parking - ¥500 flat rate
  • 👶 Facilities: Beach showers, changing rooms, family restrooms
  • Bring: Change of clothes, towel, sunscreen
  • 💰 Budget: ¥2,000-3,000 (tower tickets + parking)
06:00-07:30 PM

🍜 Dinner: Hakata Ramen Experience

Duration: 1.5 hours | Authentic tonkotsu ramen!

  • Ichiran Ramen (Canal City): Famous solo booth dining - kids get their own booth! (¥1,200/person)
  • Ippudo Hakata: Family tables, english menu, kid-friendly (¥1,000-1,500/person)
  • Kids order: Half ramen (~¥600) + gyoza (~¥300)
  • Ramen customization: Spice level, noodle firmness, richness - all customizable!
  • Side dishes: Gyoza (¥350), fried rice (¥400), karaage (¥450)
  • 💰 Budget: ¥3,500-4,500 for family
08:00 PM

🏨 Return to Hotel

Evening walk, bath time, rest for tomorrow's aquarium adventure!

💡 Pro Tips for Day 2:
  • Weather: June is rainy season - bring umbrella/raincoat (foldable ones at ¥100 shops)
  • Swan boats: Go early (10-11 AM) to avoid crowds, sun less harsh
  • Parking hack: Use Times/Coin parking apps for best rates
  • Beach safety: No lifeguards at Momochi - supervise kids closely
  • Sun protection: June sun is strong - hat, sunscreen (SPF 50+), sunglasses
  • Tower skip: If running late, skip tower and enjoy beach/sunset instead

💰 Day 2 Total Budget: ¥10,000-15,000 (~S$100-150)

Essential Travel Tips

🚗 Driving in Japan

  • Drive on the LEFT side
  • Get International Driving Permit before trip
  • GPS: Request English GPS with rental car
  • Tolls: Highway tolls expensive (¥3,000-5,000 for longer trips)
  • Gas: Say "man-tan" for full tank
  • Parking: Most attractions have parking ¥300-800

👧 Traveling with Kids

  • Request child car seat from rental company
  • Convenience stores have clean bathrooms everywhere
  • Parks have excellent playgrounds - take breaks!
  • Many restaurants have kids menus
  • Coin laundry widely available
  • Bring snacks - kids get hungry!

☔ June Weather

  • Rainy season (tsuyu) - expect rain!
  • Pack rain jackets & umbrellas
  • Temperature: 20-28°C (68-82°F)
  • Humid - light, breathable clothing
  • Sunscreen still needed
  • Indoor backup activities planned

💴 Money & Payment

  • Cash is king - many places don't take cards
  • 7-Eleven ATMs accept foreign cards
  • Budget: ¥15,000-20,000/day for family
  • IC card (Suica/Pasmo) useful for trains
  • Tax-free shopping at major stores (passport needed)

📱 Connectivity

  • Rent pocket WiFi at airport
  • Or get tourist SIM card
  • Download Google Maps offline
  • Google Translate app is essential
  • Most hotels have free WiFi

🎒 Packing Essentials

  • ✅ Sunscreen and hats
  • ✅ Rain gear (jackets, umbrella)
  • ✅ Comfortable walking shoes
  • ✅ Swimwear (beach & onsen)
  • ✅ Small backpack for day trips
  • ✅ Portable phone charger
  • ✅ Basic first aid kit
