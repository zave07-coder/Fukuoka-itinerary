# Fukuoka Family Itinerary
**Dates:** June 14-23, 2025
**Travelers:** 2 adults + 1 child (6 years old)
**Transportation:** Rental car available

---

## Day 1: June 14 (Saturday) - Arrival & Settling In
- **Arrive in Fukuoka**
- Pick up rental car at airport
- Check into hotel
- Evening: Light exploration of nearby area
  - **Dinner suggestion:** Canal City Hakata (shopping/dining complex with fountains - kids love it!)
  - Easy first night, combat jet lag

---

## Day 2: June 15 (Sunday) - Fukuoka City Highlights
- **Morning:** Ohori Park
  - Beautiful Japanese garden
  - Rent swan boats on the pond (kids love this!)
  - Playground area for your daughter
- **Lunch:** Nearby cafe or picnic
- **Afternoon:** Fukuoka Castle Ruins at Maizuru Park
  - Short walk, great views
- **Evening:** Nakasu/Tenjin area
  - **Dinner:** Try Fukuoka's famous Hakata ramen at Ichiran or Ippudo

---

## Day 3: June 16 (Monday) - Marine World & Beach
- **Morning-Afternoon:** Marine World Uminonakamichi
  - Aquarium with dolphin/sea lion shows
  - Very kid-friendly
  - About 30 min drive from central Fukuoka
- **Afternoon:** Uminonakamichi Seaside Park
  - Right next to the aquarium
  - Huge park with playgrounds, flower fields, animal petting zoo
  - Rent bikes or take the park train
- **Evening:** Return to Fukuoka city
  - Rest and casual dinner

---

## Day 4: June 17 (Tuesday) - Road Trip to Yufuin & Beppu
- **Drive to Yufuin** (1.5-2 hours from Fukuoka)
  - Charming hot spring town with mountain views
  - Lake Kinrinko - peaceful lake walk
  - Browse cute shops on Yunotsubo Kaido street
  - **Lunch:** Local restaurant
- **Afternoon:** Continue to Beppu (30 min from Yufuin)
  - Visit "Hells of Beppu" (Jigoku Meguri) - colorful hot spring viewing spots
  - Umi Jigoku (Sea Hell) and Chi no Ike Jigoku (Blood Pond Hell) are highlights
  - Kids find these fascinating!
- **Option 1:** Stay overnight in Beppu at a family-friendly onsen ryokan
- **Option 2:** Drive back to Fukuoka (1.5 hours)

---

## Day 5: June 18 (Wednesday) - Beppu or Return Day
**If stayed in Beppu:**
- Morning: Relax at onsen hotel, try family bath
- Drive back to Fukuoka after late checkout
- Afternoon: Rest, laundry, regroup

**If returned to Fukuoka:**
- Relaxed day in the city
- **Visit:** Fukuoka Anpanman Children's Museum (perfect for 6-year-old!)
- **Afternoon:** Momochi Seaside Park & Fukuoka Tower
  - Beach area, playground
  - Tower has observation deck with city views

---

## Day 6: June 19 (Thursday) - Dazaifu Day Trip
- **Drive to Dazaifu** (30-40 min from Fukuoka)
- **Morning:** Dazaifu Tenmangu Shrine
  - Beautiful historic shrine
  - Walk through traditional shopping street (umegae mochi rice cakes - delicious!)
- **Nearby:** Kyushu National Museum
  - Interactive exhibits, family-friendly
  - Cultural learning experience
- **Afternoon:** Komyozenji Temple
  - Small zen temple with beautiful gardens
- **Return to Fukuoka**
- **Evening:** Tenjin Underground Shopping or Hakata Station area

---

## Day 7: June 20 (Friday) - Road Trip to Karatsu & Beaches
- **Drive to Karatsu** (1 hour from Fukuoka)
- **Morning:** Karatsu Castle
  - Seaside castle with ocean views
  - Relatively small, not overwhelming for kids
- **Nearby:** Nijinomatsubara Pine Grove
  - Scenic coastal pine forest, easy walking
- **Afternoon:** Beach time!
  - Niji no Matsubara Beach or nearby beaches
  - Your daughter can play in the sand
- **Late afternoon:** Return to Fukuoka
- **Evening:** Explore Hakata Gion area

---

## Day 8: June 21 (Saturday) - Nokonoshima Island
- **Morning:** Drive to Meinohama Ferry Terminal
- **Take ferry to Nokonoshima Island** (10 min ferry ride - kids love ferries!)
- **Island Park:** Nokonoshima Island Park
  - Flower fields (should be beautiful in June!)
  - Petting zoo with rabbits and goats
  - Playground areas
  - Athletic field and activities
  - Amazing views of Hakata Bay
- **Lunch:** On the island (local seafood, BBQ area)
- **Afternoon:** More island exploration or beach time
- **Return:** Ferry back, evening in Fukuoka
- **Dinner:** Try monja-yaki or okonomiyaki (interactive dining fun for kids!)

---

## Day 9: June 22 (Sunday) - Flexible Day / Favorites Revisit
- **Option 1:** Kawachi Fujien Wisteria Garden road trip (if interested in scenic drives)
  - About 1 hour drive
  - Wisteria may be finished but gardens still pretty

- **Option 2:** Revisit favorite spots from the week
  - Return to beach or park your daughter loved most

- **Option 3:** Yanagawa Day Trip
  - 1 hour drive south
  - River boat rides through old canal town (very relaxing and scenic)
  - Eel restaurants (unagi is specialty here)

- **Evening:** Final night dinner - your choice of favorite Fukuoka food
  - Pack and prepare for departure

---

## Day 10: June 23 (Monday) - Departure
- **Morning:** Last-minute shopping
  - Hakata Station for souvenirs
  - Canal City if you want more variety
- Return rental car at airport
- **Departure**

---

## Family-Friendly Restaurant Suggestions
- **Hakata Ramen:** Ichiran, Ippudo, Shin Shin
- **Mentaiko (spicy cod roe):** Try it on rice or pasta
- **Motsunabe:** Hot pot (some restaurants have private rooms)
- **Conveyor belt sushi:** Fun for kids! (Genki Sushi, Kappa Sushi)
- **Tempura:** Tempura Hirao (Hakata)
- **Yatai food stalls:** Evening street food experience (some are kid-friendly)

---

## Tips for Traveling with a 6-Year-Old in Fukuoka
1. **Convenience stores:** 7-Eleven and Family Mart have clean bathrooms and snacks
2. **Parks:** Japan has excellent playgrounds - take breaks!
3. **Coin laundry:** Many available if needed
4. **Car seats:** Ensure rental car company provides appropriate child seat
5. **Parking:** Most attractions have parking; download Japan parking apps
6. **Food:** Many restaurants have kids' menus or will serve plain rice
7. **Weather:** June can be rainy (rainy season) - bring rain gear!
8. **Sunscreen:** Important for beach days

---

## Accommodation Suggestions
- **Fukuoka City:** Stay in Hakata or Tenjin area for convenience
  - Hotel recommendations: Hilton Fukuoka Sea Hawk, Grand Hyatt Fukuoka, or apartment-style hotels
- **Beppu (if overnight):** Family ryokan with private onsen baths

---

## Road Trip Notes
- **Driving:** Japan drives on the left side
- **Highway tolls:** Can be expensive - budget accordingly (ETC card rental available)
- **GPS:** Most rental cars have English GPS
- **Scenic routes:** Coastal roads are beautiful!
- **Gas stations:** Full service available; say "man-tan" for full tank

---

## Packing Essentials
- Sunscreen and hats
- Rain jackets/umbrellas (June is rainy season)
- Comfortable walking shoes
- Swimwear for beaches and onsen
- Small backpack for day trips
- Portable Wi-Fi or SIM card for navigation
